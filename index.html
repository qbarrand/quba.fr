I am Quentin Barrand, a French IT student
learning Computer Science & Networks at ENSIIE and TELECOM SudParis with an apprenticeship at SNCF.

I am keen on designing and optimizing large-scale web services using open-source stacks.

I am now completing my studies at ENSIIE Paris and TELECOM SudParis, two IT engineering schools.
I will graduate in September 2016.
During this cursus, from 2013 to 2016, I held a Junior Systems & Networks Engineer position at SNCF Réseau, the french national railway company, as part of my apprenticeship. I was especially responsible of the overhaul of the embedded information system of IRIS 320, a special high speed train dedicated to railway measurement.
I am also closely following the OpenStack project, on which I had my first experience during my Summer Student internship at CERN in 2013. Since then, I have been studying the Nova scheduler and the implementation of Software-Defined Networking in OpenStack; my last semester of studies particularly focused on telecommunications, with a full course on SDN and NFV.
